Emergency
🚨 For life-threatening emergencies, call 911 immediately.
🫁
Infant Choking
For babies under 1 year
›
1
Call 911 immediately if baby becomes unresponsive.
2
Hold face-down on your forearm, head lower than chest. Support the head.
3
Give 5 firm back blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand.
4
Flip face-up. Give 5 chest thrusts: 2 fingers on the breastbone just below the nipple line.
5
Check the mouth. Remove any clearly visible object — never do blind finger sweeps.
6
Repeat until the object dislodges or help arrives.
⚠️ Never do blind finger sweeps — you risk pushing the object deeper.
🌡️
Fever
Temperature above normal range
›
1
Take temperature rectally (most accurate for infants).
2
Under 3 months: ANY fever above 100.4°F (38°C) → call the doctor immediately.
3
3–6 months: fever above 102°F (38.9°C) → call the doctor.
4
Dress lightly. Offer plenty of fluids — breast milk, formula, or water if over 6 months.
5
Infant acetaminophen or ibuprofen (over 6 months) can help — follow the dosing chart.
⚠️ Febrile seizures can occur with rapid temperature rise. If baby seizes, call 911.
🤕
Fall / Head Injury
After a drop or impact to the head
›
1
Stay calm. Assess: is baby conscious, breathing, and responding?
2
Call 911 if: unconscious, seizures, repeated vomiting, or won't stop crying after 10 minutes.
3
Do NOT move baby if you suspect neck or spine injury.
4
If alert and crying (a good sign), apply a cold pack wrapped in cloth to any bumps.
5
Monitor closely for 24 hours. Keep baby awake for 1–2 hours after the fall.
💡 A bump (goose egg) is reassuring — swelling outside the skull, not inside.
🔴
Allergic Reaction
After new food or substance exposure
›
1
Watch for: hives, swelling of face/lips/tongue, vomiting, breathing difficulty.
2
Mild reaction (hives only) → call your doctor and monitor closely.
3
Severe reaction (anaphylaxis) → call 911 immediately.
4
If prescribed: use the EpiPen at the first sign of severe reaction.
5
After EpiPen: go to ER even if symptoms improve — a second wave can occur.
⚠️ Anaphylaxis moves fast. When in doubt, call 911.
❤️
Infant CPR
Baby is unresponsive and not breathing
›
1
Check response: tap the foot and call the baby's name loudly.
2
Call 911 and put on speaker while you start CPR.
3
Lay baby on a firm flat surface. Tilt head back slightly to open airway.
4
Give 2 gentle rescue breaths: cover both mouth AND nose. Watch for chest rise.
5
30 chest compressions: 2 fingers, centre of chest, push 1.5 inches at 100–120/min.
6
Repeat 30:2 ratio until baby responds or help arrives.
📋 Take a certified infant CPR class — hands-on practice is essential.

Design Principles

Laws of UX applied in Dad Pal

Fitts
Fitts's Law
Time to reach a target = function of distance and size. All interactive elements are ≥ 44px — the iOS HIG minimum and Fitts-optimal for thumb-reach zones.
Hick
Hick's Law
Decision time grows logarithmically with options. Dad Pal uses five tabs (not more), short filter chip sets, and progressive disclosure — show the important, hide the rest.
Jakob
Jakob's Law
Users spend most of their time in other apps. Dad Pal uses native iOS patterns — tab bars, grouped lists, swipe-to-go-back — so every interaction is already familiar.
Miller
Miller's Law
Working memory holds 7 ± 2 items. Content is chunked into named sections (Today, Feeds, Milestones). No list exceeds 8 items without pagination or grouping.
Peak
Peak-End Rule
People judge experiences by their peak and their end. Milestone celebrations animate with a pop and a toast. Completing a feeding log shows warm confirmation — these moments are the peak.
Von R
Von Restorff Effect
Distinctive items are remembered. The emergency button is visually isolated — red background, high contrast, separate card — so it stands apart in any moment of panic.
Zeig
Zeigarnik Effect
Incomplete tasks stay in memory longer. The milestones progress bar (9 of 16) creates a natural pull to complete. Pending items show unfilled circles that invite a tap.
400ms
Doherty Threshold
Productivity soars when response time is under 400ms. All screen transitions are 280ms. Tap feedback (active state dimming) is 140ms. Toast notifications appear within 50ms of action.
Æsth
Aesthetic-Usability Effect
Beautiful interfaces are perceived as easier to use. Dad Pal follows Apple HIG precisely — 0.5px separators, SF Pro, 13px radius cards, backdrop blur nav bars — because craft signals trustworthiness.

Reference: lawsofux.com · Apple Human Interface Guidelines · WCAG 2.1
